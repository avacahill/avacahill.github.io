Ava Cahill

Comparative cognitive scientist interested in the evolution of cognition

I am a PhD student in Psychology at Johns Hopkins University interested in comparative cognition, theory of mind, and the evolutionary roots of social intelligence.

My research focuses on how animals interpret the behavior of others, with particular interests in canids, domestication, and communicative cues.

I use behavioral experiments to explore how species understand attention, knowledge, and social information.
