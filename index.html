About me

Comparative psychologist
curious about what dogs know.

I'm a first-year PhD student in the Department of Psychological and Brain Sciences at Johns Hopkins University, where I work in Dr. Christopher Krupenye's lab. My research centers on comparative cognition — how different species perceive, learn from, and communicate with others.

My undergraduate work at Boston College focused on how domestication has shaped dogs' responses to communicative cues, comparing them with dingoes. That work became my honors thesis and a manuscript currently under review.

Outside the lab I'm a wilderness first aider, former radio DJ at WZBC 90.3 FM, and graphic designer.
